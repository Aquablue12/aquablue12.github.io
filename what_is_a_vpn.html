What is a VPN?

Posted by user John Velasco


The first time I heard the term VPN I was in a state of complete and utter confusion. I had no idea what a VPN was. Granted that was a few years ago, and the use of VPNs has become slightly more widespread, but still, it doesn't hurt to clear up the topic.

Virtual Private Networks, more commonly known as “VPNs,” are a major asset in protecting one’s privacy on the internet. At its most basic, a VPN makes it look like your computer is connecting to somewhere where it is not. It does this by connecting to the VPN’s server, and through the server, connecting to the website. Say for example the VPN’s server is in like Belgium. If we were to use the VPN on a site that was trying to get our info, i.e our IP address, then it would just get the VPNs instead of our actual IP. We remain anonymous, and we can continue safely on the internet.

Protecting your data from being scraped by malicious websites is not the only thing VPNs can do for you. Since IP is literally an internet address, its based on your actual address (i.e. 173.166.164.121 is from Washington, D.C.), meaning they can mask your location as well, allowing you to have access to content that is region blocked. Want to watch Avatar: The Last Airbender on Netflix? VPNs got you! VPNs are just a major benefit to anyone who cares about their privacy online!
